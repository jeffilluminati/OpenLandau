For a fixed $j$, the separation between terms with different $l$ (for example, $L_{\mathrm I}$ from $L_{\mathrm{II}}$, or $M_{\mathrm I}$ from $M_{\mathrm{II}}$) results from the departure of the field acting on the inner electrons from the Coulomb field of the nucleus, that is, from allowing for the electron's interaction with the other electrons. Such doublets are called \emph{screening doublets}.
