When relativistic effects are taken into account, terms with different $j$ separate from one another (compare the discussion in \S~72 of the fine structure of the hydrogen levels). Thus, for example, $L_{\mathrm I}$ and $L_{\mathrm{II}}$ separate from $L_{\mathrm{III}}$, while $M_{\mathrm I}$ and $M_{\mathrm{II}}$ separate from $M_{\mathrm{III}}$ and $M_{\mathrm{IV}}$. Pairs of levels of this kind are called \emph{relativistic doublets}.
