Denote by $\mathbf p_0$ the momentum of the center of mass of the pair of emitted nucleons, and by $\mathbf p$ the momentum of their relative motion. Suppose that $p\ll p_0$, so that the relative energy $E=p^2/m$

\SourcePage{737}{740}
($m$ is the nucleon mass) is small in comparison with the energy of the center-of-mass motion, $E_0=p_0^2/4m$. At the same time, suppose that $E_0$ is large in comparison with the energy $\varepsilon$ of a level (real or virtual) belonging to the two-nucleon system. In other words, only the relative motion of the nucleons is assumed to be ``slow''; the nucleons themselves are ``fast.''
