If the coupling of the orbital angular momentum to the axis is weak compared with the spin--orbit coupling, this is called \emph{case $c$}. It occurs in molecules containing a rare-earth atom. Such atoms have $f$ electrons whose angular momenta are not compensated; their interaction with the molecular axis is weakened because the $f$ electrons lie deep within the atom. Types of coupling intermediate between $a$ and $c$ occur in molecules composed of heavy atoms.
