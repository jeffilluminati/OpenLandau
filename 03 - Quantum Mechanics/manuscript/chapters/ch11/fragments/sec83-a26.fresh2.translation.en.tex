The different rotational levels are characterized by values of the quantum number $J$, the molecule's total angular momentum, which includes the electrons' orbital and spin angular momenta and the rotational angular momentum of the nuclei\footnote{As is customary, the notation $K$ is reserved for the molecule's total angular momentum when its spin is excluded. In case $a$, the quantum number $K$ does not exist, since the angular momentum $\mathbf K$ is not conserved even approximately.}.
