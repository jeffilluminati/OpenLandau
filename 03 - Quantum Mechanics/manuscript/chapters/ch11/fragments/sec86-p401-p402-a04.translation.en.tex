Accordingly, all molecular terms fall into two classes: \emph{positive} terms, whose wavefunctions remain unchanged when the signs of the electron and nuclear coordinates are reversed, and \emph{negative} terms, whose wavefunctions change sign under inversion.\footnote{We follow the established terminology. It is unfortunate, since for an atom the behavior of terms under inversion is described by their parity, rather than by a sign.

For $\Sigma$ terms, the sign discussed here must not be confused with the $+$ and $-$ signs written as superscripts!}
