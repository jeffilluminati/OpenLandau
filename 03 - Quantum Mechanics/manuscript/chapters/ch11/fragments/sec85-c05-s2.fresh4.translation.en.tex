An analogous situation can arise for terms with $\Omega=0$ that, as the nuclei approach one another, turn into a molecular term with $\Lambda\ne0$ (and hence $\Sigma=-\Lambda$); these levels are doubly degenerate, since in case $a$ the terms $0^+$ and $0^-$ (which may originate from different pairs of atomic states) correspond to the same energy\footnote{Here we neglect the so-called $\Lambda$-doubling (see \S~88).}.
